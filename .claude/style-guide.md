1. Voice and Tone:
   * Authoritative yet Accessible: Write like a veteran commander. Use clear, direct instructions. Avoid "fluff" but lean into historical and mechanical weight.
   * Active Voice: Use active verbs to drive engagement.
     * Weak: "The enemy tank was spotted by the scout."
     * Strong: "Your scout spots the enemy tank."
   * Professionalism: Even when discussing gameplay, maintain a clean, technical standard. Avoid excessive slang (e.g., use "Destroyed" instead of "Rekt").
 
2. Terminology Standards

Consistency is critical for game mechanics. Use the official names for UI elements and gameplay systems.

| Category | Preferred Term | Avoid |
|----------|----------------|-------|
| Players | Commander, Tanker, Player | User, Pilot |
| Damage | Alpha Damage, DPM | Hit points lost, Punch |
| Armor | Effective Armor, Angling | Thickness, Tilting |
| Status | Knocked out, Destroyed | Dead, Killed, Broken |
| Movement | Hull down, Side-scraping | Hiding, Wall-rubbing |

Vehicle Classifications: Always capitalize vehicle classes: Light Tanks, Medium Tanks, Heavy Tanks, Tank Destroyers, and SPGs (Self-Propelled Guns).
 
3. Formatting and Mechanics
   * Numbers and Math
     * Percentages: Always use the % symbol (e.g., 5% boost to crew skills).
     * Decimals: Use two decimal places for accuracy in dispersion or reload times (e.g., 0.34m at 100m).
     * Ranges: Use an en-dash for ranges: 250–300m.
   * UI Elements: Bold any text that refers to a button, tab, or specific in-game menu. Example: "Navigate to the Research tab and select the 75 mm Gun M3."
 
4. Instructional Structure
   * When explaining how to master a specific maneuver, use a logical sequence:
     * Position the Hull:
       * Step 1. Park your tank behind solid cover (a building or rock) so your front is hidden.
     * Angle the Armor:
       * Step 2. Reverse at a slight angle (20–30°) so only your side armor is visible to the enemy.
     * Bait the Shot:
       * Step 3. Let the enemy fire at your highly-angled side. The shot will likely ricochet.
     * Counter-Attack:
       * Step 4. Pull forward while they are reloading to fire a clean shot before returning to cover.
  
5. Visual Standards: Documentation should always include visual aids for armor layouts and "Weak Spots."
   * Armor Heatmaps: Green indicates high penetration chance; Red indicates a probable bounce.
   * Module Highlights: Use standard colors—Orange for Crew, Yellow for Modules (Ammo Rack, Engine).
